orking Well
Strong foundation: Your 7 building blocks concept gives structure and prevents bad schedules due to missing data.

Flexible configuration: The ability to load templates or do custom setup is great for power users.

Error prevention focus: Your system is heavily invested in validation, which is a major plus.

Multiple entry points (Quick Actions vs Guided Workflow) helps different user types.

⚠️ What Could Be Improved
1. Too Many Steps Before Seeing Anything
Users want feedback quickly. If they need to complete 6–7 steps just to see a schedule, some may bounce.

Suggestion:
Add a “Quick Start” Scheduling Mode:

Choose an age group or division

Upload or type in team names

Pick a date range + available fields

Hit “Generate Draft Schedule”

Let the system auto-fill the rest with sensible defaults (game durations, brackets, spacing). Let users edit after seeing something concrete.

2. Combining Related Steps
Some steps can be collapsed for clarity.

Example:

Step 3 (Game Metadata) and Step 4 (Age Groups & Divisions) could be part of one "Game Rules & Divisions" step.

Steps 5 & 6 (Field/Venue & Tournament Parameters) could be a single “Venue Setup & Preferences” section.

3. Terminology May Intimidate Users
Words like “bracket logic structure,” “field capacity analysis,” “building blocks” sound technical. They’re accurate—but can be daunting to non-technical users.

Suggestion: Re-label with clearer language:

“Game Slot Rules” → “Game Timing Rules”

“Bracket Logic” → “Division Play Style”

“Tournament Parameters” → “General Settings”

4. Too Much Freedom Early
Giving the user the choice of 4 different configuration modes (quick config, config wizard, enhanced workflow, manual) before they’ve generated a single schedule may create decision paralysis.

Suggestion:
Have one simple button:

Start Scheduling

Once they generate a schedule, then offer:

“Edit Game Rules”

“Adjust Divisions”

“Advanced Config”

This gives them immediate value before the complexity.

5. Better Visual Guidance
A lot of this sounds like forms, dropdowns, and checkboxes. What could help:

Timeline UI: “You are here → Step 3: Game Rules”

Progress Indicators: “Ready to schedule: ✅ Fields ✅ Divisions ❌ Game rules”

Inline AI Suggestions: “We recommend a 7v7 format based on your U9 teams.”
